Fraud Prevention and Protection Tips

Fraud vs Non-Fraud: Guidelines

What is a Fraudulent Transaction?

  • Unexpected or unfamiliar transactions that you do not recognize.
  • Large purchases that you did not authorize, especially if it's outside your usual spending pattern.
  • Multiple transactions from a location you don't recognize, or a different country.
  • Transactions made with unfamiliar payment methods (e.g., a new credit card, digital wallet, etc.).
  • Frequent refunds or chargebacks on your account, especially if you're not requesting them.

What is a Non-Fraudulent Transaction?

  • Transactions that you recognize and authorized, such as regular subscriptions or routine purchases.
  • Purchases made from locations where you regularly shop or do business.
  • Stable payment methods (e.g., credit cards, bank transfers) that are linked to your account and you regularly use.
  • Reasonable and familiar amounts for goods or services purchased, in line with your normal spending habits.
  • Notifications or confirmations for your transactions that match your usual behavior (e.g., purchase receipts).

How to Protect Your Account from Fraud

1. Use Strong Passwords: Always use complex, unique passwords for all your accounts. Avoid reusing passwords across multiple sites, and consider using a password manager.
2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Use two-factor authentication wherever possible. It adds an additional layer of security, especially for financial accounts.
3. Monitor Your Transactions Regularly: Review your account statements and transactions regularly. Set up transaction alerts to keep track of any unusual activity.

5. Beware of Phishing: Be cautious of unsolicited emails, messages, or phone calls asking for sensitive information. Always verify the sender and avoid clicking suspicious links.
6. Update Security Settings: Ensure your accounts are using the latest security features, such as strong encryption and security questions that only you can answer.
7. Use Secure Networks: Avoid accessing your financial accounts over unsecured Wi-Fi networks. Consider using a Virtual Private Network (VPN) to protect your privacy.
8. Regularly Update Your Software: Keep your operating systems, browsers, and apps updated with the latest security patches. This reduces the risk of malware or other vulnerabilities.
